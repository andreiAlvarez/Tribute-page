Linus Torvalds

“Software is like sex : it's better when it's free.”

Here's a time line of Linus Torvalds's life:

    1969 - Born into the world.

    1996 - Graduated at the University of Helsinki.

    1991 - Obtained MINIX OS, Version 0.01, GNU + Linux Kernal.

    1996 - Started Work at Tansmeta.

    1996 - Tux is suggested.

    1996 - Linux Trademark Disputes.

    1998 - Linus makes the cover of Forbes.

    1999 - offered stock options by Red Hat.

    2001 - Just for Fun released.

    2002 - Bitkeeper causes controversy.

    2004 - Moved to Oregon.

    2005 - Creates "Git".

    2006 - Cumulatively contributed 2% of the Linux.

    2006 - Torvalds does not support GNU v3.

    2012 - Torvalds was inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame.
